Technical Details: Image Compression Explained

Image compression works by removing unnecessary data from image files. There are two main types:

Lossless compression: Preserves all image data (best for PNG with limited transparency)

Lossy compression: Removes some data for smaller files (ideal for JPG/WebP photos)

Our image kb converter automatically selects the best approach based on your target size and original image type. The algorithm:

  • Analyzes image content to preserve important details
  • Progressively reduces quality until target size is reached
  • Optionally resizes dimensions if needed to hit small targets
  • Converts between formats when beneficial for size reduction

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a limit to how much I can reduce an image?

While you can set very small targets (like 10KB), extremely aggressive compression will noticeably reduce quality. For best results, aim for reductions of 50-80% of the original size.

Will my images lose quality?

Some quality loss is inevitable when reducing file size, but our image optimizer minimizes visible artifacts. The tool shows before/after comparisons so you can evaluate the results.

What's the difference between KB and MB?

1 MB (megabyte) = 1000 KB (kilobytes). Our tool works in KB for precise control over small size reductions important for web and email use.

Are my images stored or shared?

No. All processing happens in your browser. Images are never uploaded to our servers, ensuring complete privacy.
